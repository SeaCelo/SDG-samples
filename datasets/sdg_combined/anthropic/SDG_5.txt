According to the latest SDG progress report from the UN Secretary-General, the world has witnessed some advancement on indicators related to changing discriminatory laws and promoting political participation and leadership. However, progress remains uneven across regions and countries. Significant challenges also remain on economic empowerment, education, and ending violence against women.  

On the legal and political front, more than half of all countries have reformed discriminatory laws in line with SDG 5, and two-thirds of countries have introduced new laws and policies promoting gender equality. Political participation and leadership of women has also steadily increased, with the global average of women in parliament reaching 24.9% in 2019, up from 19% in 2015. However, women's political representation remains very low in some regions like Northern Africa, Western Asia, and Oceania. Government initiatives to promote women in leadership roles will need to accelerate to achieve SDG targets.

Economic and educational empowerment of women continues to lag. The gender wage gap persists at 16% globally. Women spend over three times as much time on unpaid care and domestic work as men, limiting their opportunities in the workforce. Only two-thirds of countries have laws mandating equal pay for work of equal value. Women also remain underrepresented in managerial and leadership positions in the private sector in all regions. On education, while most regions have reached gender parity in primary education enrollment, disparities remain in secondary and tertiary education, especially in sub-Saharan Africa, Northern Africa, and Western Asia. Promoting skills training, access to financing, and more equitable sharing of domestic responsibilities are critical to improving women's economic opportunities and educational outcomes.

Violence against women remains widespread, with nearly one in three women experiencing physical or sexual violence at some point in their lives. Conflict settings exacerbate the risks of gender-based violence. Despite increased efforts to prevent and respond to violence against women, impunity for perpetrators persists in many countries and access to justice and services for survivors continues to be limited. Urgent action is needed to enforce laws, strengthen institutions, and change harmful social norms and stereotypes. 

In summary, the world is making progress on SDG 5 but at an insufficient pace. Renewed political will and commitments, significantly increased financing, and stronger policy and programmatic actions on all fronts are essential to achieving gender equality and empowering all women and girls within this decade. Partnerships across governments, civil society, and the private sector are key to advancing SDG 5 across all regions and spurring progress on this critical goal.
